Nitroglycerine

In this section we will not only talk about nitroglycerine but other explosives based on it, specifically blasting gelatin and dynamite.
In summary nitroglycerine can be made in home by nitrating glycerine with nitration acid. Blasting gelatin is mixture of nitrocellulose and nitroglycerine and dynamite is nitroglycerin soaked into a porus material such as starch or silica.
Nitroglycerine is extremely sensitive and dangerous but blasting gelatin and dynamite is much safer, both being invented by Alfred Nobel.
Nitroglycerine is not possible to produce at home in high amounts without turning it into a factory with expert workers and safety equipment.
That's why in my previous bomb making recipes, I have always talked about using low explosives because you can't make enough of them to use in bombs.
In this recipe we will talk about making low amounts of nitroglycerine and so others.
As nitroglycerin is complex to make, I recommend you to watch this:Nitroglycerine.
Now let's talk about making blasting gelatin. It's not actual gelatin. It's just looks like it. You need dissolve 7 to 13% of the weight of the nitroglycerine worth of nitrocellulose in the nitroglycerin and slowly and carefully stir it.
Your blasting gelatin is ready but to properly fo this process you must have prior chemistry knowledge and experience working with sensitive chemicals.
Next we will be talking about a simple method of making dynamite.
This is more easier to execute but may produce a wealer dynamite.
You need around 30% nitroglycerin, and 70% starch (percentage by weight) and then mix them slowly and you get dynamite.
All of these needs a lab, proper safety gear and prior experience and knowledge of chemistry to make.

Mercury Fulminate

Mercury Fulminate with the chemical formula of Hg(CNO)2 is a highly sensitive and toxic primary high explosive.
It was used mainly for making primers and blasting caps.
It's sensitivity and easy degradation over time made it obsolete.

Picric Acid

Picric acid (2,4,6-trinitrophenol) is a yellow, crystalline organic compound used used primarily in laboratory staining, synthesis, and historically as a high explosive.
While stable when wet,dry picric acid is highly friction- and shock-sensitive explosive, forming unstable metal salts too.

Dry Ice Bomb

Dry Ice Bombs are made by putting dry ice and water in a plastic container, for example a plastic bottle.
First get a big plastic container (atleast a 2L bottle) then fill the half of the bottle with water and then add lots of dry ice and close the bottle.
After some time the bottle will explode.
This happens because in water the dry ice subliminates and produces carbon dioxide which creates extreme pressure on the container and it explodes.
If you added some sharpnels in the water too, that would be deadly.
Dry Ice Bomb in general is a unpredictable and dangerous to use device and can cause serious injuries even to the user.

Handmade Grenade

Handmade Grenade can be made using a can like of condensed milk or similar cans of canned foods.
The can is obviously open so first weld it then attach a funnel to its mouth and funnel low explosives into it and also add shrapnels like nails, razor blades, bearing etc from the mouth.
My recommended combination is same as the pressure cooker bomb shrapnel combination.
Finally add a visco fuse to and seal the mouth with duct tape but the fuse must remain outside.
You may attach a stick to it with tape for better range.
If you only use nails as shrapnels it would be a nail bomb too.
You can also drill a hole from behind to put the fuse and low explosives so you can seal the mouth with welding properly because in the former mentioned method we can just use duct tape or any other strong tape for sealing.
But remember, in both processes, the welding must be done before putting low explosives in the can.
